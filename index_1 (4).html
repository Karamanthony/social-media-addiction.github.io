What are the Effects and Disadvantages of Social Media Addiction?

  • Cutting down on natural back-and-forth conversation and face-to-face communication.
  • Decreasing the connection between people by reducing the quality of the content of their conversation.
  • Having negative influence on parenting where the parents are physically present but their attention is absorbed elsewhere.
  • Creating conflicts when people expect others to be connected 24/7 on their phones and reply to them instantly.
  • Increasing people's insecurities by watching all the public figures with no faults.
  • Causing accidents in situations where full concentration should be put instead.

How do you know if you have social media addiction?

The events of this year and the various lockdown and quarantine rules imposed by countries has caused social media use to soar – 47% of internet users across 17 countries admitted their time spent on social media has increased.

Seven signs of social media addiction:

  • The first thing you do in the morning is check social media
  • You check social media during the work day
  • You are anxious when you cannot check your social media
  • You are constantly checking how your posts perform
  • You spend a lot of time overthinkg and planning your posts
  • You think you hear your phone buzz, when really it didn't
  • You neglect your own hobbies in favour if scrolling

How can you decrease social media use?

Whether you have social media addiction or are just on your apps more than you need to be, the good news is there are ways you can help decrease your overall use.

  • Turn off your personal phone during work, as well as during school, meals, and recreational activities. You can also adjust the setting on each social media app so you can turn off certain notifications.
  • Set aside a certain amount of time dedicated to social media per day. Turn on a timer to help keep you accountable.
  • Leave your phone, tablet, and computer out of your bedroom.
  • Take up a new hobby that’s not technology-related. Examples include sports, art, cooking classes, and more.
  • Make it a point to see your friends and family in person when possible.
